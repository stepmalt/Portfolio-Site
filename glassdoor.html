Team Members: Stephanie Maltese, Iris Wang, Rachel Zhao, Jiayi Huang, Lan Wang

Project Managers: Stephanie Pompelio (Senior Manager, Product Design), and Lauren Lindeen (Senior Lead User Experience Researcher)

Methods: User Interviews, Usability Testing, Heuristic Evaluation, Surveys, Comparative Analysis, Affinity Mapping

Timeline: 11 Weeks

Due to a non-disclosure agreement (NDA), I am unable to share specific details or findings from this project.

About

During the semester I took SI 622 - Needs Assessment and Usability Evaluation at the University of Michigan, my team partnered with Glassdoor for a semester-long project, applying UX research methods to enhance the platformâ€™s user experience. Our focus was on optimizing the job details section, where we analyzed the needs and priorities of various user groups. As part of this effort, I contributed to creating surveys, conducting comparative and heuristic evaluations, and facilitating user interviews to uncover challenges and gather insights for interface improvements. We also planned and executed usability tests, synthesizing the results into actionable recommendations to improve user interaction and overall effectiveness within the job details section. This project allowed me to work closely with my team to address complex user needs while delivering research-driven solutions.

Takeaways

This project highlighted the importance of adapting research methods to real-world constraints and stakeholder needs. Collaborating with a team on such a high-impact platform taught me how to navigate challenges, prioritize effectively, and approach problem-solving with empathy. Beyond the technical skills I honed, I gained a deeper appreciation for the collaborative nature of UX research and its ability to drive meaningful change in digital experiences.
